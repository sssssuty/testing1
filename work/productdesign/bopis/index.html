Problem - Why We Started?
Shipping costs, whether for initial delivery or returns, have always been a significant expense for retailers, particularly in light of the need for careful cost management during the pandemic and early post-pandemic period.

Prompted by the marketing and data teams, the larger retail team began investigating this issue more closely.
Research
Strategies Used By Other Retailers

#1 Optimize Supply Chain and Logistics: Streamlining the supply chain, including optimizing inventory management, negotiating better shipping rates, and using data analytics for demand forecasting.

#2 Leverage Store Inventory for Online Orders: Leveraging physical store inventory to fulfill online orders reduces travel distance for products, thus saving on shipping expenses and time.

#3 Offer Buy Online and Pick Up In Stores: By allowing customers to order online and pick up in-store, retailers can reduce shipping costs and bridge the gap between online and physical shopping experiences.

#4 Offer Buy Online and Ship From Stores: Similar to BOPIS, BOSHIP involves shipping online orders to a local store for pickup, reducing logistics costs associated with home delivery.

#5 Use Data Analytics for Efficient Routing: Utilizing data analytics for efficient routing optimizes shipping routes and regional warehousing, lowering both costs and delivery times.

#6 Encourage In-Store Returns: For online purchases, encourage customers to return items to the store. This can drive foot traffic while saving on return shipping costs.

Solution
Buy Online and Pick Up in Store
After evaluating the strategies and examine the team's bandwidth, we decided to proceed with Buy Online and Pick Up In Store service. Simultaneously, we would mention In Store Return more frequently in marketing materials. If the BOPIS launch was successful, the team will plan to launch Buy Online and Ship From Store service to further optimize the operation.

Targeting local customers with geolocation.
"People don't hate ads. They hate bad ads."

We want to delivery the message to the right audiences. Collaborated with the product manager and the development team, we implemented geolocation targeting to show the BOPIS banner to local customers only.

The example shown below is the skinny top banner on the homepage of MoMA Design Store's website. Other site visitors will see our evergreen banner, mentioning the museum membership and the new loyalty program.
Blending the new service into every step.
We mentioned the service from every level of the online and store experience: homepage, product category page, product detail page, shopping cart, and checkout page.

The example shown below is the product detail page, where customers can check if the desired item is available for BOPIS and if so, from which New York City store. When they add the item to the cart, a pop-up will show up, further emphasizing the service.
Next Step
Adjust the payment logic.
Due to the team's limited bandwidth, the checkout process was initially built with basic functionality.

While customers can complete BOPIS (Buy Online, Pick Up In Store) purchases when all items are available at the same location, they cannot do so if items are spread across different stores.

The system should not only allow customers to proceed with purchasing items from multiple locations but also notify them that they will need to pick up items from different stores before confirming the purchase.
