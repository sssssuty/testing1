Overview
Due to covid and large inventory delay issues, retail stores struggled to keep up the retention rate. Particularly, MoMA Design Store's customer return to purchase rate consistently stayed below the industry average of 14.5%. Also, customers treated the online and in-store experiences as separate entities, leading to fragmented journeys and diminished loyalty.

Recognizing the situation, the marketing team saw this as an opportunity to rethink our retention strategy and initiated the project.
Project Goals
Boost customer retention and engagement by offering tailored rewards and incentives.
Provide a seamless shopping experience across all digital and physical touchpoints.
Set MoMA Design Store apart from the museum experience, creating a distinct identity.
Strategic Value
The team concluded that a loyalty program would help achieve the goal.

Retailers often use loyalty programs to boost customer retention and sales by offering rewards and personalized experiences. These programs provide valuable data on consumer behavior, enabling targeted marketing and product improvements. They also foster brand loyalty, driving increased spending, positive word-of-mouth, and opportunities for customer feedback, cross-selling, and upselling.

Objectives & Approach
In the beginning of this project, we identified several key objectives that needed to be addressed to ensure a successful market introduction. Throughout the process, I kept an iterative approach and always think about our customers.
Objective 1. Build the new program with the least amount of effort, yet well-built.
In addition to a tight timeline, the team needed to manage the project alongside the busy holiday season.

1.1 Identify Valuable Touchpoints
As co-lead on the project alongside a customer retention specialist, I began by identifying the most valuable touchpoints to ensure a well-rounded service. My goal was to understand which team members needed to be involved in the preparation process to deliver a comprehensive, well-built program.
Unlike previous product launches or events, this loyalty program required coordination across the entire Retail department and awareness from the Museum department. While my partner handled the detailed logistics of perks and rewards, my focus shifted to refining the online and in-store customer experiences.
1.2 Evaluate different user journey flows.
In the early stages, the design team and I mapped out various user flows and corresponding information architecture to gauge the level of effort required.
The team ultimately selected the final plan, as it achieved the project’s core goals while minimizing the workload for the team.
1.3 Allocate work between internal teams and external partners.
To prevent overwhelming our internal teams, we brought on three external partners to assist with project execution. They helped verify the user flow, reviewed visual and copy content, and conducted testing to ensure the platform’s stability.
1.4 Accessibility is vital for a well-considerated experience.
Aligned with MoMA's nationwide accessibility guidelines, we ensured that our digital pages were well-structured and user-friendly. Additionally, we prepared in-person support for customers in stores to further enhance accessibility.
