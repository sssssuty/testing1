Problem - Why We Started?
Due to covid and large inventory delay issues, retail stores struggled to keep up the retention rate. Particularly, MoMA Design Store's customer return to purchase rate consistently stayed below the industry average of 14.5%.

Also, customers treated the online and in-store experiences as separate entities, leading to fragmented journeys and diminished loyalty.

Recognizing the situation, we saw this as an opportunity to rethink our retention strategy.
Research
Our Current Situation

Like many omni-channel retailers, customers can make purchases at the MoMA Design Store's online website, physical stores in North America, Asia, and Europe, or through wholesale orders.

As a division under the Museum of Modern Art, customers with museum memberships enjoy exclusive benefits, such as early access to launches and discounts.

In addition to seasonal sales where customers can enjoy up to 60% off, MoMA Design Store curates seasonal pop-up storesabout three times a year, each around different themes, to bring good design to our audiences.

User Research

Our research, which included over 80 email surveys and in-person user sessions, revealed a gap between customers' appreciation for our product and their willingness to purchase at full price. The data also indicate that most customers are eager to learn more about MoMA Design Store's exclusive launches and are interested in receiving personalized content.
Market Analysis

Many retailers leverage loyalty programs to enhance customer retention and drive sales by offering rewards and personalized experiences. These programs yield valuable insights through data collection on consumer behavior, enabling more targeted marketing strategies and product enhancements. Additionally, they cultivate a sense of community and brand loyalty, leading to increased spending, positive word-of-mouth, and opportunities for customer feedback, cross-selling, and upselling.
Solution
Building Connections Through a Loyalty Program
We set out to design the MoMA Design Store Rewards program — a comprehensive loyalty initiative aimed at deepening customer engagement.

By merging digital and physical shopping environments, we envisioned a seamless customer experience where rewards would not only incentivize purchases but also foster a sense of community.

Through personalized recommendations and meaningful rewards, we aimed to build long-lasting relationships with art and design lovers who are passionate about the MoMA brand.

Integrating the program at every touchpoint.
Here we present a comprehensive map of every interaction point our customers may experience with our service, encompassing both digital and physical realms. Within these varied touchpoints, we Integrated distinct content tailored to achieve specific objectives.

The touchpoints highlighted in green are strategically designed to include segments that actively promote our loyalty program, aiming to entice customers to join. Meanwhile, the areas marked in orange are focused on content that motivates customers to make purchases, effectively utilizing the benefits offered by the loyalty program.

To emphasize the benefits of the loyalty program and highlight the incentives, we created content for each touchpoint highlighted in orange.
Streamlining in-store and online redemptions.
While points can be automatically redeemed for online purchases, customers require additional identification to enjoy their perks. To streamline the process, we developed a email redemption system that allows customers to redeem their rewards in-store by presenting a unique code sent to their email.
Visual Identity
My team started with two visual directions for the brand identity. One direction highlights MoMA Design Store’s core mission: “to bring good design into people’s lives by putting it into their homes and hands.” The other concept conveys the visual representation of a loyalty program, stacking perks. After two rounds of review with the larger team, the second option was chosen to revise and refine.
