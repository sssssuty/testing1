Overview
Pathway is a passion project initiated by Sri Pakhala and I. We were inspired to derive deep into the field of finance management for international students in the U.S. because of personal struggles.

During the four months of research and design, we conducted 40+ interviews with international students, recent graduates, financial advisors, and university staff to understand the challenges and opportunities in this space. We identified four key opportunities for different groups of stakeholders, which led to the creation of Pathway.
Objective
Deepen understanding of finance and the financial management journey of international students and recent graduates in the US.
Identify and analyze pain points in financial literacy for international students and recent graduates in the US.
Ideate and design solutions to help international students in the U.S. navigate early-stage financial management.
Strategic Value
Financial literacy is a life-long journey for everyone no matter where they are based. However, for international students and recent graduates in the U.S., the journey is often more challenging due to cultural differences, language barriers, and lack of resources. It is meaningful to create a solution that guides them through the early stages of financial management, helping them build a strong foundation for their future.

Challenges
01. Bare Minimal Understanding of Finance
The reason why Sri and I are interested in this topic is because we both have little understanding of finance when we first came to the U.S.. We wanted to understand how other international students are navigating through this journey.
02. Broad Scope
Even before doing desktop research, we are aware that finance is a broad topic. We needed to narrow down our focus to make sure we are not overwhelmed by the amount of information.
03. Tight Timeline & Funding
We took the opportunity to proceed this project for a class, which means we have a tight timeline to conduct research, design, and test our solution plus little assitant with funding.

We conducted two phases of user interview sessions. The first phase is to go as wide as possible - understand the graduates' complete finance journey starting from the first year of their academic program until now (3-6 years). Reflection
01. Deepened My Understanding of Finance Management
Through this project, I gained a much deeper understanding of the financial challenges that international students and recent graduates face in the U.S. I developed insights into how they navigate early-stage financial decisions, including managing student loans, retirement plans, passive income and building credits.
02. Strengthened My Research Skills
This project allowed me to significantly enhance my research skills by employing a variety of methods, including surveys, user interviews, co-design activities, expert reviews, competitive analysis, and contextual inquiry. By combining these approaches, I was able to gather comprehensive insights into the financial challenges faced by international students and recent graduates.
03. Strengthened My Rapid Prototype Skills
Throughout this project, I honed my rapid prototyping abilities to quickly ideate, test, and iterate on solutions, and present prototypes that effectively communicated design ideas, allowing for early feedback and continuous improvement.
