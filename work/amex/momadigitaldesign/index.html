Overview
Due to covid and large inventory delay issues, retail stores struggled to keep up the retention rate. Particularly, MoMA Design Store's customer return to purchase rate consistently stayed below the industry average of 14.5%. Also, customers treated the online and in-store experiences as separate entities, leading to fragmented journeys and diminished loyalty.

Recognizing the situation, the marketing team saw this as an opportunity to rethink our retention strategy and initiated the project.
Project Goals
Boost customer retention and engagement by offering tailored rewards and incentives.
Provide a seamless shopping experience across all digital and physical touchpoints.
Set MoMA Design Store apart from the museum experience, creating a distinct identity.
Strategic Value
A loyalty program was projected to boost MoMA Design Store’s retention and sales, by offering personalized rewards and perks that enhance customer engagement. It will also provide valuable data on consumer behavior, enabling targeted marketing and product improvements.

Objectives & Approach
In the beginning of this project, we identified several key objectives and constrains that needed to be addressed to ensure a successful project development. Throughout the process, I kept an iterative approach and always think about our customers.
01. Build the well-considered program with the least amount of effort.
In addition to a tight timeline, the team needed to manage the project alongside the busy holiday season.
Assess the team's capacity and plan support in advance.
As co-lead on the project alongside a customer retention specialist, I began by understanding the team's bandwidth during the holiday season, the busiess time of all retail businesses.

My goal was firstly to understand which team members needed to be involved in which stage of the program preparation and where do we need additional resources. Secondly, to "book" team members's capacity ahead of time. To ensure time is wisely planned, we also proceed with a two-week sprint schedule.
Highlighted with the filled person icon, we identity the limited resources around these tasks and seeked additional help. We also booked time with the teams highlighted in purple ahead of time to collaborate with them.
Evaluate different user flows.
In the early stages, the design team and I mapped out various user flows for different scenarios, and corresponding information architecture to gauge the level of effort required. Showed below two options for two scenarios, as examples.
After presenting this to the development team, we learned that editing content on the My Account page is significantly more challenging than on the Rewards landing page, which the team is about to build. This is because the My Account page shares the same platform as the museum's backend system, making it far more complex to manage.
With this understanding, I decided to maximize the Rewards landing page with a logged out version and a logged in version, and only show a simple progress block on My Account page.
Reusable modules for different content and pages.
To further lessen the workload, I designed a few design elements that can be reused for different content on different pages. This way, the dev team only need to spend the minimal time on creating elements, and the design and marketing team can help editing and uploading content to the pages.
02. Bridge the online and in-store shopping experience.
Our customers might want to redeem their credits when in-store, and we needed to ensure a seamless experience for them.
Unlike online shopping, where loyalty credits are automatically applied at checkout, our customers need to present a unique coupon code for verification purposes to redeem their credits. We offer two convenient redemption options: through the website or by scanning an in-store QR code.
03. Differentiate MoMA Design Store Rewards with MoMA museum membership.
The museum membership has been a long-standing program, and the new program needed to be distinct and not to be confused with the museum membership.
Although MoMA museum members enjoy perks when making purchases at MoMA Design Store, MoMA Design Store Rewards is the loyalty program under the Retail Division and has little to do with the museum membership. Reflections
01. Personal Growth in Leadership.
Leading a new program for the first time was both challenging and enlightening. The experience taught me the importance of stakeholder management, working closely with external partners, freelancers, and facilitating effective communication and collaboration across departments. By leveraging diverse perspectives from my team and external contributors, we navigated complex challenges and delivered high-quality solutions within tight deadlines. This collaborative approach not only improved our outcomes but also enhanced my leadership skills, teaching me to balance speed with quality while adapting strategies as new information emerged.
02. Work in Parallel
We could have initiated the development process while conducting testing and redesigning in parallel, rather than waiting for all the testing and design phases to be completed. This would allowed us to adapt as the development team made new progress. For future projects, I would embrace the lean startup approach more.
